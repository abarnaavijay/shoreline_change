# Shoreline and Land Use Change Analysis in Cochin (2003–2023)

## Overview

This project investigates the **shoreline dynamics and land use/land cover (LULC) changes** in **Cochin (Kochi), Kerala**, over a 20-year period (2003–2023) using Remote Sensing and GIS techniques. Multi-temporal Landsat imagery was utilized to assess coastal changes, urban expansion, and shoreline evolution, while the **Digital Shoreline Analysis System (DSAS v5.0)** was employed to quantify shoreline erosion and accretion.

The study integrates supervised image classification with statistical shoreline change analysis to provide insights into the impacts of urbanization and natural coastal processes on one of India's most dynamic coastal regions.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze land use and land cover changes from 2003 to 2023.
- Detect shoreline changes using multi-temporal satellite imagery.
- Quantify shoreline erosion and accretion using DSAS.
- Evaluate long-term coastal dynamics to support sustainable coastal management.

---

## Study Area

**Cochin (Kochi), Kerala, India**

- Southwest coast of India
- Arabian Sea coastline
- Major commercial port city
- Rapidly urbanizing coastal region

---

## Methodology

The project followed the workflow below:

1. Collection of Landsat satellite imagery (2003–2023).
2. Generation of False Colour Composite (FCC) images.
3. Selection of training samples for supervised classification.
4. Land Use/Land Cover classification using the Maximum Likelihood algorithm.
5. Post-classification change detection and area estimation.
6. Shoreline extraction from multi-temporal datasets.
7. Baseline and transect generation using DSAS v5.0.
8. Shoreline change analysis using:
   - Linear Regression Rate (LRR)
   - End Point Rate (EPR)
   - Net Shoreline Movement (NSM)

## Results

The analysis revealed significant land use transformations over the past two decades, primarily driven by urban expansion and changes in open land and vegetation. Shoreline analysis identified both erosion and accretion hotspots along the Cochin coast. Using DSAS, the maximum erosion rate was estimated at **−10.81 m/year**, while the maximum accretion rate reached **3.59 m/year**. Statistical indicators including **LRR**, **EPR**, and **NSM** provided a comprehensive assessment of shoreline dynamics, supporting informed coastal zone management and environmental planning.
